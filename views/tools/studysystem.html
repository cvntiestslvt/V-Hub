Methods
Feynman
When: complex mechanisms
Explain as if teaching a 10-year-old. No jargon. Where you reach for jargon = the gap. Go back, fill only that gap, simplify again.
Test: can you explain this to a patient in Filipino-English?
Blurting
When: new concepts
Blank page. Write everything from memory. Open notes — highlight only what you missed. Review gaps only. Never re-read the whole thing.
Test: how many gaps did you find? That's the real assessment.
Question-first
When: Gizmo card creation
Before reading: write 3 questions you expect the section to answer. After reading: close the book. Answer all three from memory. Gaps → cards.
Test: can you answer before looking?
Spaced rep
When: daily + ongoing
Day 0: test before ending session. Day 1: Gizmo before anything new. Day 3+: Gizmo algorithm. Day 7: weekly blurt on one page. Honest ratings only.
Rule: marking Easy to clear the queue destroys retention.
Gizmo card rules
1
One card = one idea. Never cram two things into one card.
2
Answer requires thinking, not reciting. Bad: "What is Assessment?" Good: "Patient BP 160/100, looks flushed. What step of the nursing process is this and what specifically gets collected?"
3
Clinical context over definition. Always frame in what a nurse would actually face.
CONCEPT: What is [concept] and why does it matter clinically?
Back: Feynman explanation + clinical consequence
SCENARIO: Patient presents with [signs]. What do you do first and why?
Back: Action + rationale in your own words
