Tools · Core System 02
Daily Structure
Sequence-based, not clock-based. Energy-adaptive. Three modes. The skeleton the whole day hangs on.
No fixed clock times — these are blocks in sequence, not times on a calendar.
Day sequence
Wake
Morning buffer — 30 min
No phone. No scrolling. Eat, hydrate, exist. Brain priming before the first block.
30m
Block I
Nursing study — 90 min
One concept. Full study loop. Notebook → Obsidian → 3 Gizmo cards. Hard stop at 90 min even when it feels good.
90m
Rest
Break — 30 min
Walk, eat, stare. No studying. No scrolling. Brain consolidating — don't interrupt it.
30m
Block II
German — 15–30 min
Duolingo, Nicos Weg, German YouTube. Low cognitive load. Non-negotiable even on bad days.
30m
Midday
Lunch + scroll — 60 min
Actual meal. TikTok, YouTube, Twitter — here only. One hour, then it closes.
60m
Block III
Nursing study — 90 min
Second concept OR Gizmo review + blurting. Alternate new learning and review days.
90m
Move
Movement — 20 min
Walk. Stretch. Afternoon retention depends on this break.
20m
Block IV
Reading — 45–60 min
Current books. No minimums. Earned by completing Block I and German first — not scheduled before them.
60m
Wind
Synthesis + shutdown — 15 min
One line in Daily/. ✓ or ✗ in tracker. Close Obsidian. No screens 30 min before sleep.
15m

90-min active recall blocks produce more consolidation than 4 hours of passive reading. The breaks aren't wasted time — they're when your hippocampus transfers to long-term memory.
Energy calibration — ask this within 30 min of waking
Can you open a book and read one paragraph right now?
High energy
Yes, immediately.
Both study blocks · German 30 min · Reading 60 min
Default mode. Don't downgrade out of habit. When focus is available, use all of it.
Medium energy
Yes, but reluctantly.
One study block · German 15 min · Reading whenever
Pick the most interesting concept. Drop the second block — one good session beats two mediocre ones.
Low energy
No, not really.
German 15 min only · Reading if possible · Honest ✗ to tracker
No studying. No guilt. Re-entry starts tomorrow — not catch-up, just Day 1.
Six rules — non-negotiable
I
Blocks are in sequence, not at fixed times
Block I at 7am or 2pm — both fine. Sequence matters, clock doesn't.
II
Hard stop at 90 minutes — especially when it feels good
The stop is what makes tomorrow's session possible. Going over once starts the precedent that ends the system.
III
Breaks are not productivity time
No Obsidian, no Gizmo, no "just one more." Walk, eat, exist. The brain is consolidating.
IV
Scroll window is lunch break only
Outside that window: phone in another room. Not silent — another room. Silent still vibrates.
V
Reading block is always last — earned, not scheduled
Block I and German must be done before the book opens. Reading first means not studying.
VI
Synthesis before sleep, not during the day
One sentence in Daily/. Takes 60 seconds. Worth more than the note it produces.
